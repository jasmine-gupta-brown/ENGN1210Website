von Mises Yield Criterion

At a material point, the elastic constitutive equation fails to hold when a yield condition is met. One of the prominent yield condition used is the Mises (named after von Mises) yield condition: \[\text{Yield occurs/starts at a material point when }\quad \bar{\sigma} = \sigma_Y\] \[\textrm{where,}\qquad \quad \bar{\sigma} = +\,\sqrt{\; \frac{1}{2}\Big\{(\sigma_{xx} - \sigma_{yy})^2 + (\sigma_{yy} - \sigma_{zz})^2 + (\sigma_{xx} - \sigma_{zz})^2\Big\} \; + \;3(\sigma_{xy}^2 + \sigma_{yz}^2 + \sigma_{xz}^2) \; }\] is the equivalent Mises stress, and \(\sigma_Y\) is the tensile yield strength (material property) of the material.

Cylindrical Coordinates

  • Yield condition
    \[\text{Yield occurs/starts at a material point when }\quad \bar{\sigma} = \sigma_y\] where, \[\bar{\sigma} = \sqrt{\; \frac{1}{2}\Big\{\,(\sigma_{rr} - \sigma_{\theta \theta})^2 + (\sigma_{\theta \theta} - \sigma_{zz})^2 + (\sigma_{rr} - \sigma_{zz})^2\,\Big\} \;+ \;3(\sigma_{r\theta}^2 + \sigma_{\theta z}^2 + \sigma_{rz}^2) \; }\] is the equivalent Mises stress, and \(\sigma_y\) is the tensile yield strength of the material.

    • Spherical Coordinates

  • Yield condition
    \[\text{Yield occurs/starts at a material point when }\quad \bar{\sigma} = \sigma_y\] where, \[\bar{\sigma} = \sqrt{\; \frac{1}{2}\{\,(\sigma_{rr} - \sigma_{\theta \theta})^2 + (\sigma_{\theta \theta} - \sigma_{\phi\phi})^2 + (\sigma_{rr} - \sigma_{\phi\phi})^2\,\}+ 3(\sigma_{r\theta}^2 \;+\; \sigma_{\theta \phi}^2 + \sigma_{r\phi}^2) \; }\] is the equivalent Mises stress, and \(\sigma_y\) is the tensile yield strength of the material.
