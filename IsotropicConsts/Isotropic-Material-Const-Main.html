Selected Useful Relations between Elastic Constants for Linear Isotropic Materials

With \(E\) as the Elastic/Young’s modulus and \(\nu\) as the Poison’s ratio,

\(G=\dfrac{E}{2(1+\nu)}\) \(K=\dfrac{E}{3(1-2\nu)}\) \(\lambda=\dfrac{E\nu}{(1+\nu)(1-2\nu)}\)

where the Shear modulus \(G\) is defined by: \(\quad \sigma_{xy} = G\, \gamma_{xy} = 2G\, \epsilon_{xy},\)

and the Bulk modulus \(K\) is defined by: \(K = -\dfrac{p}{\epsilon_V} = -\dfrac{p}{\frac{\triangle V}{V_0}}\quad\), where \(p\) is mechanical/hydrostatic pressure.
\(\lambda \;\) is Lameś constant.

Cylindrical stress-strain relations for isotropic materials

Strains from stress: \[\begin{aligned} \epsilon_{rr} &= \frac{1}{E}\, [\sigma_{rr} - \nu(\sigma_{\theta \theta} + \sigma_{zz})]\\ \epsilon_{\theta \theta} &= \frac{1}{E}\, [\sigma_{\theta \theta} - \nu(\sigma_{rr} + \sigma_{zz})]\\ \epsilon_{zz} &= \frac{1}{E} \,[\sigma_{zz} - \nu(\sigma_{\theta \theta} + \sigma_{rr})]\\ \epsilon_{r\theta} &= \frac{1}{2G}\, \sigma_{r\theta} = \epsilon_{\theta r}\\ \epsilon_{\theta z} &= \frac{1}{2G} \,\sigma_{\theta z} = \epsilon_{z\theta}\\ \epsilon_{rz} &= \frac{1}{2G} \,\sigma_{rz} = \epsilon_{zr} \end{aligned}\] Stress from strains: \[\begin{aligned} \sigma_{rr} &= \lambda (\epsilon_{rr} + \epsilon_{\theta \theta} + \epsilon_{zz}) + 2G \epsilon_{rr}\\ \sigma_{\theta \theta} &= \lambda (\epsilon_{rr} + \epsilon_{\theta \theta} + \epsilon_{zz}) + 2G \epsilon_{\theta \theta}\\ \sigma_{zz} &= \lambda (\epsilon_{rr} + \epsilon_{\theta \theta} + \epsilon_{zz}) + 2G \epsilon_{zz}\\ \sigma_{r\theta} &= 2G\epsilon_{r\theta}\\ \sigma_{\theta z} &= 2G\epsilon_{\theta z}\\ \sigma_{rz} &= 2G\epsilon_{rz} \end{aligned}\]

Spherical stress-strain relations for isotropic materials

Strains from stress: \[\begin{aligned} \epsilon_{rr} &= \frac{1}{E} \,[\sigma_{rr} - \nu(\sigma_{\theta \theta} + \sigma_{\phi \phi})]\\ \epsilon_{\theta \theta} &= \frac{1}{E}\, [\sigma_{\theta \theta} - \nu(\sigma_{rr} + \sigma_{\phi \phi})]\\ \epsilon_{\phi \phi} &= \frac{1}{E} \,[\sigma_{\phi \phi} - \nu(\sigma_{\theta \theta} + \sigma_{rr})]\\ \epsilon_{r\theta} &= \frac{1}{2G} \,\sigma_{r\theta} = \epsilon_{\theta r}\\ \epsilon_{\theta \phi} &= \frac{1}{2G} \,\sigma_{\theta \phi} = \epsilon_{\phi \theta}\\ \epsilon_{r\phi} &= \frac{1}{2G} \,\sigma_{r\phi} = \epsilon_{\phi r} \end{aligned}\] Stress from strains: \[\begin{aligned} \sigma_{rr} &= \lambda (\epsilon_{rr} + \epsilon_{\theta \theta} + \epsilon_{\phi \phi}) + 2G \epsilon_{rr}\\ \sigma_{\theta \theta} &= \lambda (\epsilon_{rr} + \epsilon_{\theta \theta} + \epsilon_{\phi \phi}) + 2G \epsilon_{\theta \theta}\\ \sigma_{\phi \phi} &= \lambda (\epsilon_{rr} + \epsilon_{\theta \theta} + \epsilon_{\phi \phi}) + 2G \epsilon_{\phi \phi}\\ \sigma_{r\theta} &= 2G\epsilon_{r\theta}\\ \sigma_{\theta \phi} &= 2G\epsilon_{\theta \phi}\\ \sigma_{r\phi} &= 2G\epsilon_{r\phi} \end{aligned}\]
