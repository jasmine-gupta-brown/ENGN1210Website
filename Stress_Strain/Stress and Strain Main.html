Simple Stress and Strain

Consider simple (uniaxial) tension/compression (stress is applied in the axial direction, and there are no stresses in transverse (perpendicular to axial) directions). For shear, consider a force parallel to the surface. \(A_0, L_0, w_0\) are the original (relaxed) cross-sectional area, axial length (along loading direction), and width in the transverse direction, respectively. \(A, \Delta L, \Delta w\) are the deformed (loaded) body cross-sectional area, change in axial length, and change in transverse width, respectively.

Stress and Strain: \[\textrm{Stress} \quad \sigma = \frac{F}{A} \approx \frac{F}{A_0} , \qquad \textrm{Normal strain} \quad \epsilon =\frac{\mathop{}\!\mathbin\bigtriangleup L}{L_0}\]

shear strain = change in angle of perpendicular lines = \(\gamma \; (\approx \tan \, \gamma)\)
Linear Elasticity: In the elastic limit (before material yield occurs).

  1. Hooke’s Law \[\begin{aligned} \textrm{Normal stress-strain relation:} \quad \sigma &= E \, \epsilon, \quad \textrm{(where $E$ is Elastic/Young's Modulus)} \\ \textrm{Shear stress-strain relation:} \quad \tau &= G \,\gamma \; (\textrm{OR} \; \sigma_{ij} = G \,\gamma_{ij}, \; \textrm{when } i\neq j) \quad \textrm{($G$ is Shear Modulus)} \end{aligned}\] Note: Tensorial strain \(\epsilon_{ij} = \frac{1}{2}\gamma_{ij}\)

  2. Poisson’s Ratio \[\nu=-\frac{\textrm{transverse strain}}{\textrm{ axial strain}}=-\frac{\mathop{}\!\mathbin\bigtriangleup w/w_0}{\mathop{}\!\mathbin\bigtriangleup L/L_0}\] Note: for incompressible materials (no volume change under deformation), \(\nu = \frac{1}{2}\)

  3. Strain Energy Density (Energy per Unit Volume) \[\begin{aligned} \textrm{Strain energy density (General): } \quad \mathcal{U} &=\int \sigma\,d\epsilon\\ \textrm{For \emph{linear elastic material}, $\mathcal{U}$ is \emph{elastic energy density stored}:} \quad \mathcal{U} &= \dfrac{1}{2}E\epsilon^2 = \dfrac{1}{2}\sigma \epsilon = \dfrac{\sigma^2}{2E}\\ \textrm{Total energy stored for the entire volume:} \quad \mathbb{U} &= \int_V \; \mathcal{U}\, dV \end{aligned}\]

    Note: In (i, ii, iii), strain \(\epsilon\) is elastic strain for the recoverable stored elastic energy (in the elastic limit, total strain is elastic strain).

Cylindrical Coordinates

Cylindrical coordinates \((r,\theta, z)\) are related to Cartesian coordinates x, y, by \[r=[x^2 + y^2]^{\frac{1}{2}}, \qquad \theta = \tan ^{-1}(y/x),\qquad z = z\] \[x = r \cos \theta, \qquad y = r \sin \theta, \qquad z = z\]

Orthonormal basis vectors \(\{\bm{e_r} , \bm{e_\theta} , \bm{e_z}\}\) in the cylindrical coordinate system are related to cartesian coordinate system by \[\begin{aligned} \bm{e_r} &= \cos \theta \bm{e_x} + \sin \theta \bm{e_y}\\ \bm{e_\theta} &= -\sin \theta \bm{e_x} + \cos \theta \bm{e_y}\\ \bm{e_z} &= \bm{e_z} \end{aligned}\]

  1. Displacement \[\textbf{u} = \textbf{x} - \textbf{X}\] \[u_r = \textbf{u} \cdot \bm{e_r}, \qquad u_\theta = \textbf{u} \cdot \bm{e_\theta}, \qquad u_z = \textbf{u} \cdot \bm{e_z}\] \[\textbf{u} = \sum_i u_i \bm{e_i} = u_r \bm{e_r} + u_\theta \bm{e_\theta} + u_z \bm{e_z}\]

  2. Strain-displacement relations \[\begin{aligned} \epsilon_{rr} &= \frac{\partial u_r}{\partial r}\\ \epsilon_{\theta \theta} &= \frac{1}{r} \frac{\partial u_\theta}{\partial \theta} + \frac{u_r}{r}\\ \epsilon_{zz} &= \frac{\partial u_z}{\partial z}\\ \epsilon_{r\theta} &= \frac{1}{2} \, \Big(\frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial u_r}{\partial \theta} + \frac{\partial u_\theta}{\partial r} - \frac{u_\theta}{r}\Big) = \epsilon_{\theta r}\\ \epsilon_{\theta z} &= \frac{1}{2} \, \Big(\frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial u_z}{\partial \theta} + \frac{\partial u_\theta}{\partial z}\Big) = \epsilon_{z\theta}\\ \epsilon_{rz} &= \frac{1}{2} \, \Big(\frac{\partial u_r}{\partial z} + \frac{\partial u_z}{\partial r}\Big) = \epsilon_{zr} \end{aligned}\]

    3. Spherical Coordinates

    Cylindrical coordinates \((r,\theta, z)\) are related to Cartesian coordinates x, y, by \[r=[x^2 + y^2]^{\frac{1}{2}}, \qquad \theta = \tan ^{-1}(y/x),\qquad z = z\] \[x = r \cos \theta, \qquad y = r \sin \theta, \qquad z = z\]

    Orthonormal basis vectors \(\{\bm{e_r} , \bm{e_\theta} , \bm{e_z}\}\) in the cylindrical coordinate system are related to cartesian coordinate system by \[\begin{aligned} \bm{e_r} &= \cos \theta \bm{e_x} + \sin \theta \bm{e_y}\\ \bm{e_\theta} &= -\sin \theta \bm{e_x} + \cos \theta \bm{e_y}\\ \bm{e_z} &= \bm{e_z} \end{aligned}\]

    1. Displacement \[\textbf{u} = \textbf{x} - \textbf{X}\] \[u_r = \textbf{u} \cdot \bm{e_r}, \qquad u_\theta = \textbf{u} \cdot \bm{e_\theta}, \qquad u_z = \textbf{u} \cdot \bm{e_z}\] \[\textbf{u} = \sum_i u_i \bm{e_i} = u_r \bm{e_r} + u_\theta \bm{e_\theta} + u_z \bm{e_z}\]

    2. Strain-displacement relations \[\begin{aligned} \epsilon_{rr} &= \frac{\partial u_r}{\partial r}\\ \epsilon_{\theta \theta} &= \frac{1}{r} \frac{\partial u_\theta}{\partial \theta} + \frac{u_r}{r}\\ \epsilon_{zz} &= \frac{\partial u_z}{\partial z}\\ \epsilon_{r\theta} &= \frac{1}{2} \, \Big(\frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial u_r}{\partial \theta} + \frac{\partial u_\theta}{\partial r} - \frac{u_\theta}{r}\Big) = \epsilon_{\theta r}\\ \epsilon_{\theta z} &= \frac{1}{2} \, \Big(\frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial u_z}{\partial \theta} + \frac{\partial u_\theta}{\partial z}\Big) = \epsilon_{z\theta}\\ \epsilon_{rz} &= \frac{1}{2} \, \Big(\frac{\partial u_r}{\partial z} + \frac{\partial u_z}{\partial r}\Big) = \epsilon_{zr} \end{aligned}\]
