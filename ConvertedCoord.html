Equations in Tensor and Vector Notation

  1. Displacement Vector: \[\textbf{u} = \textbf{x} - \textbf{X}\] Where x and X are the position vectors for a material point in the current/deformed configuration and reference/undeformed configuration respectively.

  2. Strain-displacement relation: \[\bm{\epsilon} = \frac{1}{2} [\bm{\nabla} \bm{u} + \bm{\nabla} \bm{u^T}],\qquad \bm{\epsilon} = \bm{\epsilon}^T,\qquad |\bm{\nabla} \textbf{u}|<<1\] Where \(\bm{\nabla}\) is the gradient operator.

  3. Stress-strain relation:
    Generalized Hooke’s Law \[\bm{\epsilon} = \frac{1}{E}\; [(1+\nu) \bm{\sigma} - \nu \; (tr\bm{\sigma})\, \bm{1}]\] Lamé Equation \[\bm{\sigma} = \lambda \; tr[\bm{\epsilon}] \, \bm{1} + 2 G \,\bm{\epsilon}\]

Equations in Cartesian Coordinate System

Coordinates: \[(x,y,z)\] Basis Orthonormal Vectors: \[\{\bm{e_x}, \bm{e_y}, \bm{e_z}\}\]

  1. Displacement \[\textbf{u} = \textbf{x} - \textbf{X}\] \[u_x = \textbf{u} \cdot \bm{e_x}, \qquad u_y = \textbf{u} \cdot \bm{e_y}, \qquad u_z = \textbf{u} \cdot \bm{e_z}\] \[\textbf{u} = \sum_i u_i \bm{e_i} = u_x \bm{e_x} + u_y \bm{e_y} + u_z \bm{e_z}\]

  2. Strain-displacement relations \[\begin{aligned} \epsilon_{xx} &= \frac{\partial u_x}{\partial x}\\ \epsilon_{yy} &= \frac{\partial u_y}{\partial y}\\\epsilon_{zz} &= \frac{\partial u_z}{\partial z}\\ \epsilon_{xy} &= \frac{1}{2} \, \Big(\frac{\partial u_x}{\partial y} + \frac{\partial u_y}{\partial x}\Big) = \epsilon_{yx}\\ \epsilon_{yz} &= \frac{1}{2}\, \Big (\frac{\partial u_y}{\partial z} + \frac{\partial u_z}{\partial y}\Big) = \epsilon_{zy}\\ \epsilon_{xz} &= \frac{1}{2} \, \Big(\frac{\partial u_x}{\partial z} + \frac{\partial u_z}{\partial x}\Big) = \epsilon_{zx}\\ \end{aligned}\]

  3. Stress-strain relations for isotropic materials
    Strains from stress: \[\begin{aligned} \epsilon_{xx} &= \frac{1}{E} \,[\sigma_{xx} - \nu(\sigma_{yy} + \sigma_{zz})]\\ \epsilon_{yy} &= \frac{1}{E} \,[\sigma_{yy} - \nu(\sigma_{xx} + \sigma_{zz})]\\ \epsilon_{zz} &= \frac{1}{E} \,[\sigma_{zz} - \nu(\sigma_{yy} + \sigma_{xx})]\\ \epsilon_{xy} &= \frac{1}{2G} \,\sigma_{xy} = \epsilon_{yx}\\ \epsilon_{yz} &= \frac{1}{2G} \,\sigma_{yz} = \epsilon_{zy}\\ \epsilon_{xz} &= \frac{1}{2G} \,\sigma_{xz} = \epsilon_{zx} \end{aligned}\] Stress from strains: \[\begin{aligned} \sigma_{xx} &= \lambda (\epsilon_{xx} + \epsilon_{yy} + \epsilon_{zz}) + 2G \epsilon_{xx}\\ \sigma_{yy} &= \lambda (\epsilon_{xx} + \epsilon_{yy} + \epsilon_{zz}) + 2G \epsilon_{yy}\\ \sigma_{zz} &= \lambda (\epsilon_{xx} + \epsilon_{yy} + \epsilon_{zz}) + 2G \epsilon_{zz}\\ \sigma_{xy} &= 2G\epsilon_{xy}\\ \sigma_{yz} &= 2G\epsilon_{yz}\\ \sigma_{xz} &= 2G\epsilon_{xz} \end{aligned}\]

  4. Equations of motion/Cauchy momentum equations (Equilibrium equations)
    \[\frac{\partial \sigma_{xx}}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial \sigma_{xy}}{\partial y} + \frac{\partial \sigma_{xz}}{\partial z} + b_x = \rho\frac{\partial^2 u_x}{\partial t^2}\] \[\frac{\partial \sigma_{xy}}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial \sigma_{yy}}{\partial y} + \frac{\partial \sigma_{yz}}{\partial z} + b_y = \rho\frac{\partial^2 u_y}{\partial t^2}\] \[\frac{\partial \sigma_{xz}}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial \sigma_{yz}}{\partial y} + \frac{\partial \sigma_{zz}}{\partial z} + b_z = \rho\frac{\partial^2 u_z}{\partial t^2}\]

  5. Yield condition
    At a material point, the elastic constitutive equation fails to hold when a yield condition is met. One of the prominent yield condition used is the Mises (named after von Mises) yield condition: \[\text{Yield occurs/starts at a material point when }\quad \bar{\sigma} = \sigma_y\] where, \[\bar{\sigma} = \sqrt{\; \frac{1}{2}\Big\{(\sigma_{xx} - \sigma_{yy})^2 + (\sigma_{yy} - \sigma_{zz})^2 + (\sigma_{xx} - \sigma_{zz})^2\Big\} \; + \;3(\sigma_{xy}^2 + \sigma_{yz}^2 + \sigma_{xz}^2) \; }\] is the equivalent Mises stress, and \(\sigma_y\) is the tensile yield strength of the material.

Equations in a Cylindrical Coordinate System

Cylindrical coordinates \((r,\theta, z)\) are related to Cartesian coordinates x, y, by \[r=[x^2 + y^2]^{\frac{1}{2}}, \qquad \theta = \tan ^{-1}(y/x),\qquad z = z\] \[x = r \cos \theta, \qquad y = r \sin \theta, \qquad z = z\]

Orthonormal basis vectors \(\{\bm{e_r} , \bm{e_\theta} , \bm{e_z}\}\) in the cylindrical coordinate system are related to cartesian coordinate system by \[\begin{aligned} \bm{e_r} &= \cos \theta \bm{e_x} + \sin \theta \bm{e_y}\\ \bm{e_\theta} &= -\sin \theta \bm{e_x} + \cos \theta \bm{e_y}\\ \bm{e_z} &= \bm{e_z} \end{aligned}\]

  1. Displacement \[\textbf{u} = \textbf{x} - \textbf{X}\] \[u_r = \textbf{u} \cdot \bm{e_r}, \qquad u_\theta = \textbf{u} \cdot \bm{e_\theta}, \qquad u_z = \textbf{u} \cdot \bm{e_z}\] \[\textbf{u} = \sum_i u_i \bm{e_i} = u_r \bm{e_r} + u_\theta \bm{e_\theta} + u_z \bm{e_z}\]

  2. Strain-displacement relations \[\begin{aligned} \epsilon_{rr} &= \frac{\partial u_r}{\partial r}\\ \epsilon_{\theta \theta} &= \frac{1}{r} \frac{\partial u_\theta}{\partial \theta} + \frac{u_r}{r}\\ \epsilon_{zz} &= \frac{\partial u_z}{\partial z}\\ \epsilon_{r\theta} &= \frac{1}{2} \, \Big(\frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial u_r}{\partial \theta} + \frac{\partial u_\theta}{\partial r} - \frac{u_\theta}{r}\Big) = \epsilon_{\theta r}\\ \epsilon_{\theta z} &= \frac{1}{2} \, \Big(\frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial u_z}{\partial \theta} + \frac{\partial u_\theta}{\partial z}\Big) = \epsilon_{z\theta}\\ \epsilon_{rz} &= \frac{1}{2} \, \Big(\frac{\partial u_r}{\partial z} + \frac{\partial u_z}{\partial r}\Big) = \epsilon_{zr} \end{aligned}\]

  3. Stress-strain relations for isotropic materials
    Strains from stress: \[\begin{aligned} \epsilon_{rr} &= \frac{1}{E}\, [\sigma_{rr} - \nu(\sigma_{\theta \theta} + \sigma_{zz})]\\ \epsilon_{\theta \theta} &= \frac{1}{E}\, [\sigma_{\theta \theta} - \nu(\sigma_{rr} + \sigma_{zz})]\\ \epsilon_{zz} &= \frac{1}{E} \,[\sigma_{zz} - \nu(\sigma_{\theta \theta} + \sigma_{rr})]\\ \epsilon_{r\theta} &= \frac{1}{2G}\, \sigma_{r\theta} = \epsilon_{\theta r}\\ \epsilon_{\theta z} &= \frac{1}{2G} \,\sigma_{\theta z} = \epsilon_{z\theta}\\ \epsilon_{rz} &= \frac{1}{2G} \,\sigma_{rz} = \epsilon_{zr} \end{aligned}\] Stress from strains: \[\begin{aligned} \sigma_{rr} &= \lambda (\epsilon_{rr} + \epsilon_{\theta \theta} + \epsilon_{zz}) + 2G \epsilon_{rr}\\ \sigma_{\theta \theta} &= \lambda (\epsilon_{rr} + \epsilon_{\theta \theta} + \epsilon_{zz}) + 2G \epsilon_{\theta \theta}\\ \sigma_{zz} &= \lambda (\epsilon_{rr} + \epsilon_{\theta \theta} + \epsilon_{zz}) + 2G \epsilon_{zz}\\ \sigma_{r\theta} &= 2G\epsilon_{r\theta}\\ \sigma_{\theta z} &= 2G\epsilon_{\theta z}\\ \sigma_{rz} &= 2G\epsilon_{rz} \end{aligned}\]

  4. Equations of motion/Cauchy momentum equations (Equilibrium equations)
    \[\frac{\partial \sigma_{rr}}{\partial r} + \frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial \sigma_{r\theta}}{\partial \theta} + \frac{\partial \sigma_{rz}}{\partial z} + \frac{1}{r} (\sigma_{rr} - \sigma_{\theta \theta}) + b_r = \rho\frac{\partial^2 u_r}{\partial t^2}\] \[\frac{\partial \sigma_{r\theta}}{\partial r} + \frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial \sigma_{\theta \theta}}{\partial \theta} + \frac{\partial \sigma_{\theta z}}{\partial z} + \frac{2}{r} \sigma_{\theta r} + b_\theta = \rho\frac{\partial^2 u_\theta}{\partial t^2}\] \[\frac{\partial \sigma_{rz}}{\partial r} + \frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial \sigma_{\theta z}}{\partial \theta} + \frac{\partial \sigma_{zz}}{\partial z} + \frac{\sigma_{zr}}{r} + b_z = \rho\frac{\partial^2 u_z}{\partial t^2}\]

  5. Yield condition
    \[\text{Yield occurs/starts at a material point when }\quad \bar{\sigma} = \sigma_y\] where, \[\bar{\sigma} = \sqrt{\; \frac{1}{2}\Big\{\,(\sigma_{rr} - \sigma_{\theta \theta})^2 + (\sigma_{\theta \theta} - \sigma_{zz})^2 + (\sigma_{rr} - \sigma_{zz})^2\,\Big\} \;+ \;3(\sigma_{r\theta}^2 + \sigma_{\theta z}^2 + \sigma_{rz}^2) \; }\] is the equivalent Mises stress, and \(\sigma_y\) is the tensile yield strength of the material.

Equations in a spherical coordinate system

Spherical coordinates \((r,\theta, z)\) are related to Cartesian coordinates x, y, by \[r=[x^2 + y^2 + z^2]^{\frac{1}{2}}\] \[\theta = \cos ^{-1}\frac{z}{x^2 + y^2 + z^2}, \qquad 0 \leq \theta \leq \pi\] \[\phi = \tan^{-1}(y/x), \qquad \qquad \quad 0 \leq \phi < 2\pi\] \[x = r \sin \theta \cos \phi, \qquad y = r \sin \theta \sin \phi, \qquad z = r \cos \theta\]

Orthonormal basis vectors in the spherical coordinate system and are related to cartesian coordinate system by \[\begin{aligned} \bm{e_r} &= \sin \theta \cos \phi \bm{e_x} + \cos \theta \sin \phi \bm{e_y} + \cos \theta \bm{e_z}\\ \bm{e_\theta} &= \cos \theta \cos \phi \bm{e_x} + \cos \theta \sin \phi \bm{e_y} - \sin \theta \bm{e_z}\\ \bm{e_\phi} &= -\sin \phi \bm{e_x} + \cos \phi \bm{e_y} \end{aligned}\]

  1. Displacement \[\textbf{u} = \textbf{x} - \textbf{X}\] \[u_r = \textbf{u} \cdot \bm{e_r}, \qquad u_\theta = \textbf{u} \cdot \bm{e_\theta}, \qquad u_z = \textbf{u} \cdot \bm{e_z}\] \[\textbf{u} = \sum_i u_i \bm{e_i} = u_r \bm{e_r} + u_\theta \bm{e_\theta} + u_z \bm{e_z}\]

  2. Strain-displacement relations \[\begin{aligned} \epsilon_{rr} &= \frac{\partial u_r}{\partial r}\\ \epsilon_{\theta \theta} &= \frac{1}{r} \frac{\partial u_\theta}{\partial \theta} + \frac{u_r}{r}\\ \epsilon_{\phi \phi} &= \frac{1}{r \sin \theta} \frac{\partial u_\phi}{\partial \phi} + \frac{\cot \theta}{r} u_\theta + \frac{u_r}{r}\\ \epsilon_{r\theta} &= \frac{1}{2} \, \Big(\frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial u_r}{\partial \theta} + \frac{\partial u_\theta}{\partial r} - \frac{u_\theta}{r}\Big) = \epsilon_{\theta r}\\ \epsilon_{\theta \phi} &= \frac{1}{2} \, \Big(\frac{1}{r\sin \theta}\frac{\partial u_\theta}{\partial \phi} + \frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial u_\phi}{\partial \theta} - \frac{\cot \theta}{r} u_\phi\Big) = \epsilon_{\phi\theta}\\ \epsilon_{r\phi} &= \frac{1}{2} \, \Big(\frac{\partial u_\phi}{\partial r} + \frac{1}{r\sin \theta}\frac{\partial u_r}{\partial \phi} - \frac{u_\phi}{r} \Big) = \epsilon_{\phi r} \end{aligned}\]

  3. Stress-strain relations for isotropic materials
    Strains from stress: \[\begin{aligned} \epsilon_{rr} &= \frac{1}{E} \,[\sigma_{rr} - \nu(\sigma_{\theta \theta} + \sigma_{\phi \phi})]\\ \epsilon_{\theta \theta} &= \frac{1}{E}\, [\sigma_{\theta \theta} - \nu(\sigma_{rr} + \sigma_{\phi \phi})]\\ \epsilon_{\phi \phi} &= \frac{1}{E} \,[\sigma_{\phi \phi} - \nu(\sigma_{\theta \theta} + \sigma_{rr})]\\ \epsilon_{r\theta} &= \frac{1}{2G} \,\sigma_{r\theta} = \epsilon_{\theta r}\\ \epsilon_{\theta \phi} &= \frac{1}{2G} \,\sigma_{\theta \phi} = \epsilon_{\phi \theta}\\ \epsilon_{r\phi} &= \frac{1}{2G} \,\sigma_{r\phi} = \epsilon_{\phi r} \end{aligned}\] Stress from strains: \[\begin{aligned} \sigma_{rr} &= \lambda (\epsilon_{rr} + \epsilon_{\theta \theta} + \epsilon_{\phi \phi}) + 2G \epsilon_{rr}\\ \sigma_{\theta \theta} &= \lambda (\epsilon_{rr} + \epsilon_{\theta \theta} + \epsilon_{\phi \phi}) + 2G \epsilon_{\theta \theta}\\ \sigma_{\phi \phi} &= \lambda (\epsilon_{rr} + \epsilon_{\theta \theta} + \epsilon_{\phi \phi}) + 2G \epsilon_{\phi \phi}\\ \sigma_{r\theta} &= 2G\epsilon_{r\theta}\\ \sigma_{\theta \phi} &= 2G\epsilon_{\theta \phi}\\ \sigma_{r\phi} &= 2G\epsilon_{r\phi} \end{aligned}\]

  4. Equations of motion/Cauchy momentum equations (Equilibrium equations)
    \[\frac{\partial \sigma_{rr}}{\partial r} + \frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial \sigma_{r\theta}}{\partial \theta} + \frac{1}{r\sin \theta}\frac{\partial \sigma_{r\phi}}{\partial \phi} + \frac{1}{r} (2\sigma_{rr} - \sigma_{\theta \theta} - \sigma_{\phi\phi} + \cot \theta \; \sigma_{r\theta}) + b_r = \rho\frac{\partial^2 u_r}{\partial t^2}\] \[\frac{\partial \sigma_{r\theta}}{\partial r} + \frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial \sigma_{\theta \theta}}{\partial \theta} + \frac{1}{r\sin \theta}\frac{\partial \sigma_{\theta \phi}}{\partial \phi} + \frac{1}{r}[ 3 \sigma_{\theta r} + \cot \theta (\sigma_{\theta \theta} - \sigma_{\phi\phi})] + b_\theta = \rho\frac{\partial^2 u_\theta}{\partial t^2}\] \[\frac{\partial \sigma_{r\phi}}{\partial r} + \frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial \sigma_{\theta \phi}}{\partial \theta} + \frac{1}{r\sin \theta} \frac{\partial \sigma_{\phi \phi}}{\partial \phi}+ \frac{1}{r} (3 \sigma_{\phi r} + 2 \cot \theta \; \sigma_{\phi \theta}) + b_\phi = \rho\frac{\partial^2 u_\phi}{\partial t^2}\]

  5. Yield condition
    \[\text{Yield occurs/starts at a material point when }\quad \bar{\sigma} = \sigma_y\] where, \[\bar{\sigma} = \sqrt{\; \frac{1}{2}\{\,(\sigma_{rr} - \sigma_{\theta \theta})^2 + (\sigma_{\theta \theta} - \sigma_{\phi\phi})^2 + (\sigma_{rr} - \sigma_{\phi\phi})^2\,\}+ 3(\sigma_{r\theta}^2 \;+\; \sigma_{\theta \phi}^2 + \sigma_{r\phi}^2) \; }\] is the equivalent Mises stress, and \(\sigma_y\) is the tensile yield strength of the material.

Some Useful Relations between Elastic Constants for Isotropic
Materials

\(G=\dfrac{E}{2(1+\nu)}\), \(K=\dfrac{E}{3(1-2\nu)}\), \(\lambda=\dfrac{E\nu}{(1+\nu)(1-2\nu)}\)
