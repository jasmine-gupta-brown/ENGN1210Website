Second Order Tensor

For a second order tensor, in index notation, subscripts i and j represent values as either {1,2,3} or {x,y, z} in the cartesian coordinate system, represent {r, \(\theta\), z} in the cylindrical coordinate system, and {r, \(\theta, \phi\)} in the spherical coordinate system. With \(\bm{A}\) as a second order (3x3) tensor (it can represent a stress or a strain tensor),

  1. Transpose of a Tensor: \(\bm{A}^T\) \[[{A}_{ij}]^T = [{A}_{ji}]\]

  2. Trace of a Tensor: \[tr\, \textbf{A} = A_{xx} + A_{yy}+A_{zz}\]

  3. Deviatoric Part or Deviator of a Tensor: \[dev \, \textbf{A} = \textbf{A}_0 = \textbf{A} - \dfrac{1}{3}\, (tr \textbf{A})\, \bm{1}\]

  4. Determinant of a Tensor:
    det\(\textbf{A}\) is determinant of \(\textbf{A}\) matrix.

    Determinant of a \(3\times 3\) matrix: \[det \begin{bmatrix} A_{xx} & A_{xy} & A_{xz}\\ A_{yx} & A_{yy} & A_{yz}\\ A_{zx} & A_{zy} & A_{zz} \end{bmatrix} =A_{xx}\,(A_{yy}\,A_{zz} - A_{yz}\,A_{zy}) -A_{xy}\, (A_{yx}\,A_{zz} - A_{yz}\,A_{zx}) +A_{xz}\, (A_{yx}\,A_{zy}-A_{yy}\,A_{zx})\] Determinant of a \(2\times 2\) matrix:
    \(det \begin{bmatrix} A_{xx} & A_{xy} \\ A_{yx} & A_{yy} \\ \end{bmatrix} =A_{xx}A_{yy}-A_{xy}A_{yx}\\ \)

  5. Eigen Values (Principal Values) and Eigen Directions (Principal Directions)
    Eigen/Principal values are solution \(\omega_i\)’s of the equation: \[det[\bm{A}-\omega\,\bm{1}]=0 \qquad \textrm{(where $\bm{1}$ is the identity tensor)}\] \[\implies det \begin{bmatrix} A_{xx}-\omega & A_{xy} & A_{xz}\\ A_{xy} & A_{yy}-\omega &A_{yz}\\ A_{xz} & A_{yz} & A_{zz}-\omega \end{bmatrix} = 0\] Note that for stress and strain, the highest eigenvalue when positive is the maximum tensile stress/strain acting at that material point.
    If \(\bm{\hat{m}}\) is the eigenvector (Principal direction) corresponding to an eigenvalue (Principal value) \(\omega_i\), then: \[[\bm{A}-\omega_i\,\bm{1}]\,\{\bm{\hat{m}}\}=\{\textbf{0}\}\] \[\begin{bmatrix} A_{xx}-\omega_i & A_{xy} & A_{xz}\\ A_{xy} & A_{yy}-\omega_i &A_{yz}\\ A_{xz} & A_{yz} & A_{zz}-\omega_i \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} m_x\\ m_y\\ m_z \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0\\ 0\\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\] \[\textrm{where } \quad m_x^2 + m_y^2 + m_z^2 = 1 \quad \textrm{since } \bm{\hat{m}} \textrm{ is a unit vector.}\]
